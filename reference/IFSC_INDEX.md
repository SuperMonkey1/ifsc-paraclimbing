## Governance & Structure Documents

### 1. IFSC Statutes
- **Filename:** `ifsc statutes.pdf`
- **Document Type:** Constitutional document / Supreme law
- **Date:** Adopted October 31, 2023 (Extraordinary General Assembly)
- **Key Topics:**
  1. Legal foundation and Swiss association law framework
  2. General Assembly powers and procedures
  3. Executive Board composition, election, and authority
  4. Membership requirements and categories (full, provisional, associate)
  5. Commission structure and independence requirements
  6. Voting rights, quorum, and majority requirements
  7. Statutory amendment procedures (two-thirds majority)

- **Relevant Sections for Paraclimbing:**
  - Article 14: Commission establishment and Terms of Reference
  - IPC recognition as governing body for Paralympic sports
  - Athletes' Commission independence (ToR approved by GA, not EB)
  - Disciplinary and Ethics Commission independence provisions
  - Representation and diversity provisions
  - Athlete voting rights in commission appointments

- **Key Defined Terms:**
  - **Statutes:** Supreme constitutional law binding all IFSC bodies
  - **General Assembly (GA):** Supreme legislative authority
  - **Executive Board (EB):** Executive governing body
  - **Member Federation:** National climbing federation with IFSC membership
  - **Quorum:** Minimum attendance for valid voting (typically 50% of members)
  - **Simple Majority:** More than 50% of votes cast
  - **Two-Thirds Majority:** 66.67% or more of votes cast (for Statute changes)
  - **Provisional Member:** New federation without full voting rights
  - **Voting Register:** Official list of federations eligible to vote

- **Cross-References:**
  - Establishes authority for all other IFSC documents
  - References Swiss Civil Code (legal foundation)
  - Mandates Strategic Plan implementation
  - Requires Terms of Reference for all commissions
  - Binds Executive Board decisions
  - Recognizes IPC authority for Paralympic sports
  - Approved with 93.75% vote (45 of 48 members)

### Venue & Infrastructure
**Primary Documents:**
- Venue Description and Walls Requirements and Guidelines ★ NEW
- Competition Regulations Para Climbing Events 2025
- IFSC Rules 2025

**Key Information:**
- Wall specifications: Boulder (4.5m height, 30m length), Lead (min 15m height), Speed (15m height, 5° overhang)
- European Standards compliance: EN 12572-1, EN 12572-2, EN 12572-3
- Para-specific requirements: wheelchair accessible walls, classification rooms
- Classification rooms: Physical Impairment (20m²), Visual Impairment (20m², IBSA standards)
- Isolation zone minimum: 450m² for Boulder/Lead/Para, 200m² for Speed
- Spectator capacity: World Championships min 5,000, World Cups min 2,000
