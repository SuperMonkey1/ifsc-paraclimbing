DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION  
1.1 
Capitalised terms used in these Statutes and not otherwise defined herein shall have the meanings assigned to 
them in Section 20 of these Statutes.  
1.2 
In these Statutes, references to:  
1.2.1 
sections and annexes are, unless otherwise specified, references to sections and annexes to these 
Statutes;  
1.2.2 
a “person” includes any person, firm, company, corporation, government, state or agency of a state or 
any association, trust or partnership;  
1.2.3 
where the context so permits, words importing the singular only shall include the plural and vice versa 
and words importing the neuter gender shall include all genders.  
 
2 
NAME, LEGAL STATUS, HEADQUARTERS AND REGISTRATION 
2.1 
The International Federation of Sport Climbing (IFSC) is the sole and exclusive international sport federation 
recognised by the International Olympic Committee (IOC) and the International Paralympic Committee (IPC) 
governing Climbing and Para Climbing competitions in all their formats and disciplines worldwide, including any 
forms of eClimbing and ePara Climbing competitions.  
2.2 
The IFSC is established for an indefinite period with legal status as an association in accordance with Articles 60 
et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code having its legal seat in Bern, Switzerland. The IFSC is a non-profit and non-
governmental international organisation.  
2.3 
World Climbing is the designation used operationally by IFSC. Any reference to or use of World Climbing in these 
Statutes or other official documents shall apply to the IFSC. 
2.4 
The operational headquarters of the IFSC are in Torino, Italy.  
2.5 
The IFSC is registered in the commercial register of the canton of Bern, Switzerland.  
 
3 
AUTHORITY  
3.1 
The IFSC is the sole and exclusive world governing authority acting in all matters connected with Climbing and 
Para Climbing as competitive sports.  
3.2 
The IFSC owns and may use the designs, logograms, symbols, and similar features identified in Annex 1 (as 
amended from time to time). The IFSC reserves all of its rights, including any and all intellectual property rights, 
in relation to the designs, logograms, symbols and similar features identified in Annex 1 (as amended from time 
to time).  
3.3 
The IFSC owns the Intellectual Property Rights in:  
3.3.1 
any and all IFSC Materials;  
3.3.2 
the IFSC Competitions, including the IFSC World Championships, the IFSC World Cups and any other 
IFSC Events; and  
3.3.3 
any other materials, events and/or property in respect of which the IFSC may acquire such interests as 
are customarily subject to intellectual property rights.

OFFICIAL LANGUAGE  
4.1 
The official language of the IFSC, including its bodies and constituents, is English.  
4.2 
All documents and communications by the IFSC and its bodies and constituents shall be drawn up in English. All 
verbal and written statements, reports, minutes, official documents, communications, and announcements of 
all IFSC bodies and events shall be in English.  
4.3 
National Federations may, at their discretion, translate IFSC official documents into their preferred language. In 
case of divergence on the wording and interpretation, the English version shall prevail.  
 
5 
VALUES AND PRINCIPLES  
5.1 
The IFSC shall perform its activities according to the highest ethical standards and shall require compliance with 
such standards from its National Federations and other stakeholders in Climbing and Para Climbing. In particular, 
the IFSC shall be bound by and comply with the values of the Olympic Charter and the Constitution and the 
Regulations of the International Paralympic Committee (IPC).  
5.2 
The IFSC shall not allow any discrimination against a Country, National Federation, person or group on account 
of race, national or social origin, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, political opinion, or other reason.  
5.3 
The IFSC shall promote the values of Climbing and Para Climbing through balanced, inclusive and representative 
governance structures.  
5.4 
The IFSC shall respect the autonomy of its National Federations.  
5.5 
The IFSC shall use reasonable efforts to carry out its activities in alignment with the relevant UN Sustainable 
Development Goals. 
5.6 
The IFSC shall fight against doping, illegal betting, and all other forms of abuse that may undermine IFSC 
Competitions and equality, health and safeguarding of the athletes.  
 
6 
OBJECTIVES  
6.1 
The objectives of the IFSC are:  
6.1.1 
to promote and grow the international development of Climbing and Para Climbing as competitive 
sports worldwide in all material respects, including eClimbing and ePara Climbing;  
6.1.2 
to promote the sport of Climbing and Para Climbing as recreational and leisure sports in the interest of 
all climbers and especially as a healthy activity for the youth; 
6.1.3 
to be a programme sport in the Olympic and Paralympic Games;  
6.1.4 
to deliver world-class international competitions showcasing all IFSC Disciplines equally;  
6.1.5 
to establish, maintain, and enforce rules for the organisation and participation in international Climbing 
and Para Climbing competitions, including eClimbing and ePara Climbing competitions;  
6.1.6 
to recognise the international competitions that comply with the IFSC Rules and Regulations;  
6.1.7 
to promote and organise the IFSC World Championships, the IFSC World Cup series and any other IFSC 
Events in all IFSC Disciplines;  
6.1.8 
to create, deliver, and maintain programmes for the continued education of Technical Officials;  
6.1.9 
to support development initiatives identified by IFSC bodies and approved by the Executive Board;

8 
MEMBERSHIP  
8.1 
Eligibility  
8.1.1 
The national institution or organisation governing IFSC Disciplines in any Country shall be eligible to be 
an IFSC-approved National Federation.  
8.1.2 
Only one (1) institution or organisation recognised by the IFSC as a National Federation and as the 
national governing body for IFSC Disciplines in a Country can become a member of the IFSC.  
8.1.3 
In order to be eligible for membership of the IFSC, the constitution and governing rules of a National 
Federation shall be compliant with the principles, values, and objectives of the IFSC. Such constitution 
and governing rules must not be in conflict with these Statutes or the IFSC Rules and Regulations.  
8.1.4 
Each National Federation shall acknowledge and respect that it shall recognise the IFSC as the only 
international organisation that governs the IFSC Disciplines.  
8.1.5 
A National Federation may have branches located in different geographical areas, provided that the 
National Federation notifies the IFSC of the identity of each such branch and that the National 
Federation assumes ultimate responsibility for each such branch.  
8.2 
Rights and Obligations of National Federations  
8.2.1 
All National Federations are entitled:  
8.2.1.1 
to have access to the services of the IFSC;  
8.2.1.2 
to appoint delegates to participate, speak and vote in the General Assembly meetings, in 
accordance with these Statutes;  
8.2.1.3 
to register athletes and participate in the IFSC World Championships, the IFSC World Cup 
series and any other IFSC Events when they are eligible to participate in accordance with 
applicable IFSC Rules and Regulations;  
8.2.1.4 
to exercise all rights of a National Federation and receive all benefits as specified in these 
Statutes and the IFSC Rules and Regulations;  
8.2.1.5 
to make proposals to change these Statutes and, where applicable, the IFSC Rules and 
Regulations and to make nominations of Candidates for the positions within the Executive 
Board and other IFSC bodies, where applicable;  
8.2.1.6 
to submit proposals for inclusion in the agenda of the General Assembly (see Section 10.6 
below);  
8.2.1.7 
to have access and benefit from IFSC development and educational programmes; and  
8.2.1.8 
to submit requests to the IFSC to organise international climbing and Para Climbing 
competitions.

8.3 
Admission of New Members  
8.3.1 
Application for membership of the IFSC shall be submitted by the Applicant with all documents 
accompanied by an English translation to the IFSC Office for approval by the General Assembly together 
with:  
8.3.1.1 
a statement of the name and legal entity of the Applicant and a confirmation of its good 
financial standing;  
8.3.1.2 
the official address and contact details of the Applicant;  
8.3.1.3 
a copy of the current constitution and rules and regulations of the Applicant in original and 
in English;  
8.3.1.4 
a list of the main officers of the Applicant;  
8.3.1.5 
a list of the Applicant’s active membership (i.e., number of clubs, athletes, coaches, 
officials);  
8.3.1.6 
a formal undertaking by the Applicant to observe and abide by these Statutes, the IFSC 
Rules and Regulations, the World Anti-Doping Code (WADC) as well as the jurisdiction and 
decisions of the CAS; and  
8.3.1.7 
confirmation of payment of a non-refundable administrative application fee as determined 
by the Executive Board;  
8.3.2 
The Executive Board shall have the power to approve an Applicant for provisional membership, subject 
to final approval by the General Assembly at its next meeting. The rights and duties of a new member 
shall come into effect upon provisional approval by the Executive Board, with the exception of the right 
to vote, which may only be exercised after admission of the Applicant by the General Assembly.  
8.3.3 
Once admitted by the General Assembly, a National Federation is a member of the IFSC for an indefinite 
period, unless it withdraws its membership, or its status is otherwise changed in accordance with these 
Statutes.  
8.3.4 
Once admitted, each National Federation is assigned to the respective Continental Council and may 
participate in all relevant continental activities. A National Federation may not be admitted as Member 
of a Continental Council until it has been admitted by the IFSC.

8.4 
Suspension of Membership  
8.4.1 
The Executive Board shall have the power to suspend the membership of a National Federation (and to 
impose such further measures as the Executive Board may deem reasonable):  
8.4.1.1 
for significant violation of these Statutes and/or the IFSC Rules and Regulations and upon 
other grounds deemed significant by the Executive Board;  
8.4.1.2 
for failing to pay the fees required to be paid by these Statutes and/or the IFSC Rules and 
Regulations;  
8.4.1.3 
upon the initiation of bankruptcy, administration, liquidation proceedings or receivership 
in relation to that National Federation; or 
8.4.1.4 
in the event of a dispute as to which national body may represent the IFSC Disciplines in a 
Country and until such dispute has been resolved in that Country.  
8.4.2 
The suspension shall have the consequences and scope deemed appropriate by the Executive Board. 
In particular, a suspended National Federation may be subject to the temporary forfeiture, total or 
partial, of the membership rights granted under these Statutes. The Executive Board shall carry out the 
necessary investigations and take the necessary measures to assist and support the suspended 
National Federation in having its membership reinstated. If no corrective action satisfactory to the 
Executive Board is achieved within reasonable time, the Executive Board may recommend the 
membership of the suspended National Federation for termination to the next General Assembly (see 
Section 8.5 below).  
8.4.3 
In case of suspension, the Executive Board may assign the sporting power to a third party (another 
National Federation or other IFSC body) at conditions the Executive Board deems appropriate in order 
to ensure that athletes from the suspended National Federation are able to compete and minimise the 
impact of such suspension on the athletes.  
8.4.4 
The decision of suspension of the Executive Board is subject to appeal to the General Assembly. The 
time limit for the appeal shall be thirty (30) days. The decision of the General Assembly confirming the 
suspension shall be subject to further appeal to CAS in accordance with Section 17 below.  
8.5 
Termination of Membership  
8.5.1 
Membership of the IFSC shall cease on the date when:  
8.5.1.1 
the National Federation ceases to exist, or  
8.5.1.2 
the membership of the National Federation is terminated by the General Assembly (see 
Sections 8.2.4 and 8.4.2 above), or  
8.5.1.3 
the National Federation withdraws its membership. A National Federation wishing to 
withdraw its membership of the IFSC must give written notice of such withdrawal to the 
Secretary General. That National Federation’s membership shall end on the date specified 
in such notice or, if no date is specified, on the date of receipt of such notice by the 
Secretary General.  
8.5.2 
In the event of such termination or withdrawal:  
8.5.2.1 
the IFSC shall have no obligation to make any refund of any kind whatsoever to the National 
Federation;  
8.5.2.2 
the National Federation shall continue to be bound by any provision in these Statutes 
and/or the IFSC Rules and Regulations that survives the termination and/or withdrawal of 
such membership;

THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY  
10.1 
General Provisions  
10.1.1 
The General Assembly of the National Federations is the highest authority of the IFSC and has ultimate 
authority over all IFSC activities.  
10.1.2 
The General Assembly shall in principle meet every year (see Section 10.3: Ordinary General Assembly).  
10.1.3 
The National Federation hosting the General Assembly shall in principle be approved at the meeting of 
the General Assembly two years in advance (see Section 10.3.7 below).  
10.2 
Powers of the General Assembly  
10.2.1 
The General Assembly has the following powers:  
10.2.1.1 
to adopt, amend and/or supplement these Statutes;  
10.2.1.2 
to approve the annual report of the Executive Board;  
10.2.1.3 
to review the annual budget and approve the audited annual accounts;  
10.2.1.4 
to grant discharge to the Executive Board;  
10.2.1.5 
to elect the Members of the Executive Board;  
10.2.1.6 
to appoint the members of the Disciplinary and Ethics Commissions upon the 
recommendation of the Executive Board;  
10.2.1.7 
to elect the independent Auditor;  
10.2.1.8 
to approve the minutes of General Assembly meetings;  
10.2.1.9 
to determine membership of National Federations;  
10.2.1.10 
to determine the annual Membership Fee;  
10.2.1.11 
to confer the title of Honorary President of the IFSC and Honorary Member of the IFSC to 
individuals. Other awards may be conferred by the Executive Board;  
10.2.1.12 
to adopt the IFSC Strategic Plan;  
10.2.1.13 
to award the IFSC World Championships;  
10.2.1.14 
to decide on proposals by the National Federations and the Executive Board; and 
10.2.1.15 
to decide on any other matters that are not conferred to any other IFSC body by these 
Statutes or the IFSC Rules and Regulations.

10.3 
Ordinary General Assembly  
10.3.1 
The Ordinary General Assembly shall take place every year, in principle before 30 June. The calling notice 
shall be sent to the National Federations at least ninety (90) days ahead of the date of the meeting, in 
case of elections at least one-hundred-twenty (120) days in advance.  
10.3.2 
The Secretary General shall notify the National Federations of the agenda for each Ordinary General 
Assembly together with the supporting documentation thirty (30) days before the meeting. A provisional 
agenda shall be circulated at least sixty (60) days ahead of the meeting. Proposals to amend these 
Statutes shall be circulated with the provisional agenda at least sixty (60) days ahead of the meeting.  
10.3.3 
The Executive Board shall determine the date of the Ordinary General Assembly and shall be responsible 
for the necessary preparations. The Ordinary General Assembly may be held in-person, by 
videoconference or by any other means of communication or a combination thereof. In principle, the 
Ordinary General Assembly is held in person, while ensuring that participation and voting is available for 
remote delegates. 
10.3.4 
The Executive Board shall make reasonable efforts to provide simultaneous translation into at least two 
languages other than English at the Ordinary General Assembly using objective criteria for choosing 
those additional two languages such as, for example but without limitation, the language of the hosting 
country and the language that is most widely used by National Federations.  
10.3.5 
The National Federations shall make reasonable efforts to attend each Ordinary General Assembly 
through their delegates. Each National Federation may register up to three (3) delegates, one of whom 
shall exercise the voting rights. If a National Federation registers more than one (1) delegate to the 
Ordinary General Assembly (in-person or remote), the IFSC encourages the second delegate to be of 
different gender. If the National Federation registers a third delegate, such third delegate shall be of 
different gender.  
10.3.6 
If compelling reasons so require, the Executive Board may postpone the date of an in-person Ordinary 
General Assembly by up to ninety (90) days or determine that the respective Assembly be held only 
virtually.  
10.3.7 
The General Assembly shall entrust the hosting and organisation of the Ordinary General Assembly with 
physical presence to a National Federation of a country capable of fulfilling the conditions specified by 
the Executive Board.  
10.4 
Extraordinary General Assembly  
10.4.1 
The Executive Board may call an Extraordinary General Assembly at any time on its own initiative or shall 
do so upon a proposal supported by the majority of the National Federations.  
10.4.2 
Any such proposal must be substantiated and be accompanied by a proposal for the agenda for such 
Extraordinary General Assembly.  
10.4.3 
The Executive Board shall establish the agenda of an Extraordinary General Assembly in accordance 
with the proposal submitted and call the Extraordinary General Assembly within ninety (90) days after 
receipt of such proposal. The Secretary General shall notify the National Federations of the agenda for 
such Extraordinary General Assembly together with the supporting documentation thirty (30) days 
before the meeting.  
10.4.4 
Any Extraordinary General Assembly shall, in principle, be held by videoconferencing. Instead of calling 
an Extraordinary General Assembly, the Executive Board may submit a proposal for Electronic Voting.

10.5 
Conduct of the General Assembly  
10.5.1 
The President shall lead the General Assembly. The President shall be entitled to participate in the

THE EXECUTIVE BOARD 
11.1 
General provisions  
11.1.1 
The Executive Board is accountable to the General Assembly and is the supreme authority of the IFSC 
between the General Assembly meetings.  
11.1.2 
The Members of the Executive Board must take office in their personal capacity and not as 
representatives of any National Federation or Continental Council. The Members of the Executive Board 
may not act as delegates, representatives, or advisors of a National Federation at the General Assembly 
or any other IFSC Event.  
11.1.3 
The President and the Executive Board Members have a fiduciary responsibility towards the IFSC and 
are subject to the IFSC Code of Conduct on Sports Betting Integrity. Any breach of this fiduciary 
responsibility may result in disciplinary sanctions.  
11.2 
Composition  
11.2.1 
The Executive Board consists of twelve (12) members, namely:  
11.2.1.1 
the President elected by the General Assembly;  
11.2.1.2 
four (4) Vice-Presidents elected by the General Assembly – two male and two female;  
11.2.1.3 
five (5) Continental Board Members elected by the General Assembly – coming from each 
of the five (5) IFSC-recognised Continents (see Section 15.1 below); and  
11.2.1.4 
two (2) members – one male and one female – elected by the Athletes’ Commission.  
11.2.2

11.2.3 
The President and the other Members of the Executive Board must belong to different National 
Federations with the exception of the representatives of the Athletes Commission.  
11.2.4

Section 15.1 below). Their role is not to represent any of the Continental Councils but the interests of 
IFSC globally while offering a geographic perspective.  
11.2.5 
The President chairs the Executive Board.  
11.2.6 
After the closing of the General Assembly at which its members have been elected, the Executive Board 
shall designate from among the four (4) Vice-Presidents one who shall be responsible for Para Climbing 
(VP for Para Climbing). Otherwise, the Executive Board shall constitute itself.  
11.3 
Powers and Responsibilities  
11.3.1 
The Executive Board takes all necessary decisions. As far as such decisions fall within the competence 
of the General Assembly, they are valid until the next General Assembly and must be ratified by the 
General Assembly at its next meeting.  
11.3.2 
The Executive Board is responsible for the strategy and the overall conduct of business of the IFSC. It 
shall deliver the objectives of the IFSC and carry out the activities through the Secretary General and the 
IFSC Office, and according to the IFSC Strategic Plan.

General Provisions

DISPUTE RESOLUTION 
17.1 
Any dispute arising out of or in relation to these Statutes, the IFSC Rules and Regulations or otherwise, between 
National Federations or between one or more National Federations and the IFSC, which cannot satisfactorily 
be resolved within the IFSC, shall be finally decided by the Appeal Arbitration Division of the Court of Arbitration 
for Sport in Lausanne, Switzerland. The time limit for the appeal shall be thirty (30) days.  
17.2 
Any decision of the Disciplinary Commission (see Section 14.4 above), including decisions on violations of the 
IFSC Code of Conduct, shall be subject to appeal to the Appeal Arbitration Division of the Court of Arbitration for 
Sport in Lausanne, Switzerland. The time limit for the appeal shall be thirty (30) days.  
17.3 
Any violation within the IFSC of the applicable anti-doping rules, including the World Anti-Doping Code (WADC), 
shall be referred to adjudication by the Anti-Doping Division of the Court of Arbitration for Sport in Lausanne, 
Switzerland (CAS ADD). The decision of the CAS ADD shall be subject to appeal to the Appeal Arbitration Division 
of the Court of Arbitration for Sport.

DISSOLUTION OF THE IFSC  
18.1 
The General Assembly may dissolve the IFSC by the majority of three quarters (3/4) of all Valid Votes cast, 
provided that at least two thirds (2/3) of the National Federations are present at the respective General Assembly.  
18.2 
If the IFSC is dissolved, the remaining capital after settlement of all outstanding debts shall be entrusted to 
another "for public benefit" utility in Switzerland (tax free declared institution) and shall be used for the objectives 
stated in Section 6 of these Statutes, particularly for climbing and Para Climbing as a popular, competitive and 
recreational sport.  
18.3 
In the circumstances described in Section 18.1, the General Assembly that dissolves the IFSC shall make such 
additional recommendations as it deems appropriate.

19.2 
Management of Finances and Compensation  
19.2.1 
The Financial Regulations shall define the principles and protocols for the management of the 
Resources. The Financial Regulations shall be approved by the Executive Board and shared with the

19.2.2 
Expenses and compensation of the President, Executive Board Members and other individuals serving 
on IFSC bodies shall be regulated by the relevant annexes to the Financial Regulations to be approved

19.3 
Indemnity  
19.3.1 
Every member of the Executive Board, every member of a Commission, the Secretary General and the 
staff of the IFSC shall be indemnified by the IFSC, to the fullest extent permitted under Swiss Law, in 
respect of any personal liability arising from any claim of whatever nature that is made against them as 
a result of their office or the exercise of their powers or the execution of their duties under these Statutes 
and/or the IFSC Rules and Regulations, provided that they can demonstrate that they have acted at all 
times in good faith.  
19.4 
Liability  
19.4.1 
Only the assets of the IFSC can be used to settle the liabilities and obligations of the IFSC. 
19.4.2 
Any liability of the IFSC for the liabilities of the National Federations is excluded.  
19.5 
Severability 
19.5.1 
Where a matter arises for which provision has not been made in these Statutes and/or the IFSC Rules 
and Regulations, the Executive Board may provide for the matter as it deems appropriate.  
19.5.2 
If any provision of these Statutes is determined to be unlawful or unenforceable for any reason, the 
remainder of these Statutes shall remain unaffected and enforceable.  
19.6 
Amendments to the Statutes 
19.6.1 
Subject to Section 19.6.2, these Statutes may only be amended, supplemented, or repealed by a motion 
passed at a General Assembly meeting.  
19.6.2 
Amendments to these Statutes may be made by the Executive Board to correct typographical, 
grammatical, or clerical errors or clarification or where those amendments arise from motions to amend 
these Statutes, provided that the amendments do not materially contradict the decisions of the General 
Assembly. 
19.7 
Notifications and Communications  
19.7.1 
All notifications and communications by the IFSC shall be validly made to the last known address of the 
recipient for whom the same is intended, as notified by that recipient.  
19.7.2 
Any such notification or communication may be made by delivery against receipt, registered post, 
courier, email, or any other means of telecommunication that provides a record of the sending thereof.  
19.7.3 
The IFSC's official medium of publication is the Swiss Official Gazette of Commerce (SOGC).

20.1 
Definitions 
 
Applicant 
means a national organisation governing IFSC Disciplines in a Country, which applies 
for Membership with the IFSC.  
Athletes Commission 
means the body representing the active athletes and providing input and advice on 
matters affecting the IFSC and the IFSC Disciplines from the perspective of the 
athletes.  
Auditor 
means a person or legal entity appointed and authorised to examine the annual 
accounts and accounting records of the IFSC.  
Ballot 
means a sheet of paper or a digital form on which a voter marks his or her vote or the 
names of Candidates for election.  
Candidate 
means an individual who runs for election as President, Vice-President or Continental 
Board Member.  
Commission  
means any group, organisation and body or committee irrespectively of its name, 
which has been established by the General Assembly, the Executive Board or the 
President in support of the objectives of the IFSC. The composition, task and 
competence of any Commission is set out in the respective Terms of Reference.  
Continents  
means the IFSC-recognised Continents specified in Section 15.1 of these Statutes.  
Country 
means a sovereign state as recognised by the United Nations and any other political 
entity acknowledged by the IFSC.  
Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) 
means the Court of Arbitration for Sport in Lausanne, which has exclusive jurisdiction 
to adjudicate disputes related to the IFSC Statutes and/or the IFSC Rules and 
Regulations either between National Federations or between National Federations and 
the IFSC and to hear appeals against final decisions of the General Assembly and the 
Disciplinary Commission.  
Disciplinary Commission  
means the dispute resolution body which hears and adjudicates breaches of the IFSC 
Rules and Regulations (with the exception of the IFSC Anti-Doping Rules) or 
misconduct reported to the IFSC. 
Electronic Voting 
means voting by email or any other form of electronic communication, whereby voters 
are asked one (1) or more specific questions and return their answer(s) by electronic 
communication or enter them into an online voting system.  
Ethics Commission 
means the commission with the task to hear cases because of alleged breaches of the 
IFSC Code of Conduct. It shall also assist in conflict of interest matters. 
Executive Board 
means the supreme authority of the IFSC between the General Assembly meetings. 
The Executive Board is accountable to the General Assembly.  
Executive Board Member  
means any member of the Executive Board entitled to vote.  
Extraordinary General Assembly 
means a General Assembly called by the Executive Board upon an urgent proposal 
supported by two thirds (2/3) of all Executive Board Members or a majority of votes of 
National Federations. Extraordinary General Assembly meetings are held by 
videoconferencing. 
General Assembly 
means the general assembly of the National Federations and the highest authority of 
the IFSC. 
Honorary Membership  
means a special award for individuals who have rendered outstanding services to the 
IFSC or the IFSC Disciplines. 
IFSC  
means the International Federation of Sport Climbing, i.e. the worldwide governing 
body of the IFSC Disciplines. The abbreviation IFSC is used in all languages.  
IFSC Anti-Doping Rules 
means the Anti-Doping Rules of the IFSC, as based on the World Anti-Doping Code and 
recognised by WADA.
